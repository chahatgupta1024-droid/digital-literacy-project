Introduction

Nowadays, many people use UPI and online payment apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, and Paytm for sending and receiving money. Online payment is fast and easy, but sometimes fraud and scams also happen. Many people lose their money because they do not know about cyber fraud. This case study is about a UPI fraud and how it happened and what we learn from it.

Case Description

Riya is a college student who wanted to sell her old phone online. She posted an advertisement on a website. After some time, a man called her and said he wanted to buy the phone. He said he would send the money through UPI.

The man sent a QR code to Riya and told her to scan it to receive money. Riya scanned the QR code and entered her UPI PIN. But instead of receiving money, ₹5000 was deducted from her bank account. The man blocked her number after that. Riya realized that she was cheated.

She then complained to the bank and cyber crime portal, but it was difficult to get the money back.

Reason of Fraud

The fraud happened because:

She scanned a QR code to receive money.
She shared her UPI PIN.
She trusted a stranger online.
She did not verify the payment request.
Preventive Measures

To prevent UPI fraud, we should:

Never share UPI PIN with anyone.
Remember: QR code is used to pay, not to receive money.
Always check the payment details before entering PIN.
Do not trust unknown people online.
Use official apps only.
Immediately report fraud on cyber crime helpline 1930.
Conclusion

UPI and online payments are very useful and time-saving, but we must be careful while using them. One small mistake can lead to loss of money. So, everyone should know about cyber fraud and follow safety rules to stay safe online.
